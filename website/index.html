Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

IT plays an integral role in every industry. It helps companies improve business processes, achieve cost efficiencies, drive revenue growth and maintain a competitive advantage in the marketplace. Information technology can speed up the time it takes new products to reach the market. Companies rely on IT for fast communications, data processing and market intelligence.

Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully

In order to make a digital project successful we need following. A vision of the product, which comes from the sponsor of the project. A development, which will translate the vision of the sponsor to a software art-effect. A UX/ Design team, which will make sure the digital product is of high quality and easy to use. A QA team, which will make sure that digital products have all the functionality as per the vision of the product sponsor. Collaboration tools will help the digital team to communicate in a productive manner. Slack is a well adopted tool.

Describe the risks that might impact digital projects

One of the biggest risk or a digital project is the scope creep, which will lead the project to not finish on time. If the user feedback is not taken regularly during the development of digital product, we may end up with a product which doesn’t solve the given problem.

Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software

Every digital project is different from the last one, hence you have to choose the tools according to the nature of the project. You should always consider choosing the tools which are widely adopted by the market.

Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

It is important to know that the internet has certain laws and regulations, which includes constraints on uploading and downloading, copyrights, licence, child protection. In 1995 GDPR came into legal force and it has far-reaching impacts on individuals and organisations in all sectors globally. The GDPR is the EU’s way of giving individuals, prospects, customers, contractors and employees more power over their data and less power to the organisations that collect and use such data for monetary gain.One of the aim of GDPR was to protect individuals’ personal data.
